Who is Padre Pio?

Padre Pio · 1918-1968

Padre Pio (May 25, 1887 – September 23, 1968), born Francesco Forgione, was an Italian Capuchin Franciscan friar. On September 20, 1918, while praying at the monastery in San Giovanni Rotondo, he received the stigmata — wounds on his hands, feet, and side matching those of Christ's crucifixion — which persisted for 50 years.

Beyond the stigmata, Padre Pio was renowned for bilocation, reading hearts, miraculous healings, and prophetic abilities. Through his letters and conversations with the faithful, he left numerous prophetic statements concerning the Church's destiny, world affairs, and humanity's moral trajectory. He was canonized by Pope John Paul II on June 16, 2002.

Padre Pio's most famous prophecies include the 'Three Days of Darkness' eschatological vision, predictions about WWII, and warnings about future popes and the fate of the Church. This archive compiles 14 traceable prophetic statements and evaluates those whose deadlines have passed.

Core Message
"Pray, hope, and don't worry. Worry is useless. God is merciful and will hear your prayer."
Key Timeline
  • 1918 — First received the stigmata during prayer, began prophetic activity
  • 1940s — Predicted the course of WWII and Italy's fate
  • 1947 — Young Karol Wojtyla visited; Padre Pio prophesied he would become Pope
  • 1959 — Series of warnings about the Church's future and moral decline
  • 1968 — Final prophecies before death, concerning Three Days of Darkness and eschatological purification
