Who is Gordon Moore?

Gordon Moore · 1965-2015

Gordon Earle Moore (January 3, 1929 – March 24, 2023) was an American chemist, businessman, and co-founder of Intel Corporation. In 1965, he published a paper in Electronics magazine observing that the number of transistors on an integrated circuit doubles approximately every two years while costs halve — a prediction that became known as 'Moore's Law'.

Moore's Law is not a physical law but an empirical industry prediction, yet it guided the semiconductor industry with remarkable accuracy for 60 years. From personal computers to smartphones, from the internet to artificial intelligence, the exponential growth in computing power predicted by Moore's Law has profoundly transformed human civilization.

Beyond his core transistor density prediction, Moore made forward-looking statements about home computers, handheld devices, self-driving cars, and more. This site compiles 14 of his key predictions and evaluates those whose deadlines have passed.

Core Message
"The number of components on an integrated circuit will double roughly every two years, while costs are cut in half. This trend should continue for at least ten years."
Key Timeline
  • 1965 — Published paper in Electronics magazine, first proposing transistor doubling rule
  • 1968 — Co-founded Intel Corporation with Robert Noyce
  • 1975 — Revised doubling period from one year to two years
  • 1995 — Predicted the internet would fundamentally change business and communication
  • 2005 — Acknowledged Moore's Law would hit physical limits within 10-20 years
  • 2015 — Intel slowed chip cadence; Moore's Law deceleration became industry consensus
