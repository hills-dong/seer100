What is Medjugorje?

Our Lady of Medjugorje · 1981至今

Medjugorje is a small village in Bosnia and Herzegovina. On June 24, 1981, six local teenagers reported seeing an apparition of the Virgin Mary on a hillside outside the village. She identified herself as the 'Queen of Peace' (Kraljica Mira), wearing a white veil and bluish-grey robe with a crown of stars around her head.

For over four decades since, the six visionaries (Vicka, Mirjana, Ivanka, Ivan, Marija, and Jakov) have continued to report messages from Our Lady. She has gradually revealed to each of them ten secrets concerning future world events, while repeatedly emphasizing five core themes: peace, faith, conversion, prayer, and fasting — known as the 'Five Stones,' like the five pebbles David used against Goliath.

On September 19, 2024, the Vatican's Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith, with Pope Francis's approval, granted a 'nihil obstat' (no objection) to the Medjugorje phenomenon, recognizing its abundant spiritual fruits — including numerous conversions, sacramental renewal, and vocations — while not pronouncing on the supernatural character of the apparitions. An estimated 40 million pilgrims have visited Medjugorje.

Core Message
"Dear children! I have come among you because God has sent me. My name is Queen of Peace. I call you to peace, fasting, and prayer."
